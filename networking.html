The Networking Process: Step by Step

1. Build Your LAMP List

The LAMP List is a strategic tool that helps you identify and prioritize 40 target organizations for your job or internship search. Instead of randomly applying, the list guides you to focus your networking efforts on companies that align with your interests, values, and opportunities. By filling in the four columns (List • Alumni • Motivation • Posting) you’ll be able to see which organizations have alumni connections, how motivated you are to work there, and whether they’re currently hiring. Once sorted, the LAMP list gives you a clear roadmap of where to start building connections and reaching out.

  • Creating a LAMP List for Strategic Networking

2. Find Alumni & Connections

After you finish your LAMP list, the next step is to go through our Intro to Making Connections slide to learn how to identify real people at those organizations and start building relationships. Focus on alumni, professionals, and anyone in your extended network who can share insights.

Where to find people

  • UCAN: UCAN (University Career Alumni Network) What is UCAN?
  • PeopleGrove: Connect with Alumni

3. Reach Out & Get a Response

Send short, clear, personalized messages. Ask for a quick 15–20 minute chat—not for a job. For templates, check out: Intro to Making Connections .

  • Networking Outreach Email Examples
  • Networking Email Templates

4. Follow-Up Strategies

Why it matters: Following up shows professionalism and appreciation.

  • Send a Thank-You Email: Within 24 hours—keep it short, genuine, and specific.
  • Connect on LinkedIn: Add a personalized note (e.g., “Great speaking with you today”).
  • Stay in Touch: Share updates or send relevant articles occasionally.
  • Track Your Outreach: Use CareerLink or spreadsheets to track contacts.

What Makes You Stand Out?

Resume Snapshot

Be ready to share a concise, action-oriented version that highlights impact (numbers, outcomes) rather than tasks.

LinkedIn Presence

Keep your photo professional and your headline clear (e.g., “UX Designer | Bridging Design & Technology”). Add recent projects and skills so people can learn more after meeting you.

Professional Presence

Timely replies, clear emails, customized outreach, and prompt thank-you notes all make a strong impression.

Portfolio / GitHub (as relevant)

Show real work—case studies, code, or prototypes that support your story and demonstrate your unique strengths.

Networking FAQ

How many people should I reach out to?

Aim for quality, not just quantity. Start with 5–10 strong connections, then expand gradually.

What if they don’t reply?

Wait 7–10 days, then follow up once politely. If still no response, move on—don’t take it personally.

Do I have to ask about jobs directly?

No. Focus on learning, building relationships, and gathering insights. Opportunities often come naturally later.

How do I balance networking with coursework?

Dedicate 1–2 hours per week. Small, consistent steps (sending 1–2 outreach messages) are more effective than cramming.
